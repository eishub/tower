Perception Tower

In the triple-tower architecture, the perception tower consists of a set of rules to derive higher-level and more complex predicates from the primitive predicates.

Primitive predicates are the properties that can be directly observed from the environment. In the case of the TRTower, the primitive predicates are On(x, y) and Holding(z). Using these two predicates, the Perception Tower derives: Clear(), Ordered(), and Tower().
